Welcome to Rīga, Latvia

The Gothic spires that dominate Rīga's cityscape might suggest austerity, but it is the flamboyant art nouveau that forms the flesh and the spirit of this vibrant cosmopolitan city, the largest of all three Baltic capitals. Like all northerners, it is quiet and reserved on the outside, but there is some powerful chemistry going on inside its hip bars, modern art centres and the kitchens of its cool experimental restaurants.

Top experiences in Rīga

Alberta iela

Enjoy the walk on Alberta street - the Art Nouveau area in the heart of the city.

It's breathtaking views will surely stay in your memories for years! Plus, you can always enjoy a cup of tea or a pint in one of the extraordinary restaurants on the street!

Vecrīga

The Old Town is a unique place in Europe: many buildings were build in 1200s! So you can learn the architecture history simply by taking a walk! There is absolutely no surprise Vecrīga was included in the Unesco World Heritage list :)

And of course, the amount of cafes and shops will never leave you unsatisfied.

Riga Central Market

Architecturally-imposing food pavilions, built in the 1920s, are Riga Central Market’s calling card. Originally used as military airship hangars, they were later transformed into market venues.

Isn't that fascinating? The market does not only offer an extraordinary range of products: food, clothes, supplies, but has played an important role in history!

Welcome to Lyon, France

Lyon might only be the 3rd biggest city of France, but it is definitely the oldest. Built by the Romans thousands of years ago, it prospered to this day and offers a wide choice of activities to visitors. Home of the famous french chef Paul Bocuse, it is definitely the gastronomic capital of France. The city is a mix of antique, renaissance and modern architecture so everyone can enjoy walking around !

Top experiences in Lyon

Roman Theatre

Discover the Roman Ruins, aqueducs, and Antique Theatre

Walk around the ruins of that giant Roman Theatre, almost as good as new. If you're interested in it's history, you can visit the museum right behinf it, it's free !

Quartier de La Confluence

This neighbourhood' architecture is certainly peculiar...You will find yourself surrounded by an environment right out of a science-fiction movie ! Visit the museum and go shop in the huge mall if you have time.

Try to spot the cheese shaped building (hint: it's orange and has holes in it, you can't miss it)!.

Lights Festival

If you come in early December, you will be right on time for the Festival of Lights ! For 3 days, artists from around the world gather to illuminate Lyon in a unique way, attracting 3 millions of people every year.

Don't be afraid to stay up late, the animations only starts when the sun comes down ;)

Welcome to Cusco, Peru

Welcome to the navel of the world. The undisputed archaeological capital of the Americas, Cuzco is the continent’s oldest continuously inhabited city and the gateway to Machu Picchu. Cosmopolitan Cuzco (also Cusco, or Qosq’o in Quechua) thrives with a measure of contradiction. Ornate cathedrals squat over Inca temples, massage hawkers ply the narrow cobblestone passages, a rural Andean woman feeds bottled water to her pet llama while the finest boutiques sell pricey alpaca knits.

Top experiences in Cusco

Machu Picchu

Nestled high in the slopes of the Andes, the ruins of Machu Picchu continue to reveal the mysteries of the Inca Empire

When an earthquake occurs, the stones in an Inca building are said to “dance;” that is, they bounce through the tremors and then fall back into place. Without this building method, many of the best known buildings at Machu Picchu would have collapsed long ago.

Plaza de Armas

In Inca times, the plaza, called Huacaypata or Aucaypata, was the heart of the capital. Today it’s the nerve center of the modern city.

Two flags usually fly here – the red-and-white Peruvian flag and the rainbow-colored flag of Tahuantinsuyo. Easily mistaken for an international gay-pride banner, it represents the four quarters of the Inca empire.

The planetarium

An excellent way to explore the fascinating Inca cosmovision. They defined constellations of darkness as well as light, used astronomy to predict weather patterns, and designed Cuzco’s main streets to align with constellations at key moments.

Watching the skies with the professional telescopes, is one of the most mind blowing experience of the tour. You will see the light of the stars that travelled years and years just to get to your eyes.
